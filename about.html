üèôÔ∏è About the New York Bee Club

The New York Bee Club (NYBC) is a community of urban beekeepers dedicated to promoting sustainable beekeeping practices in New York City. Founded by passionate apiarists, we provide education, support, and resources for both new and experienced beekeepers.

Our Mission

  • Promote responsible urban beekeeping
  • Support bee conservation efforts
  • Educate the public about bee importance
  • Foster community among NYC beekeepers

What We Do

  • Monthly meetings and workshops
  • Swarm removal and relocation services
  • Beginner beekeeper mentorship
  • Advocacy for bee-friendly policies

üìä Contributing to Bee Science

Every marker you add contributes to a growing database of urban bee activity. This data helps researchers understand:

  • Seasonal patterns of swarming behavior
  • Preferred nesting sites in urban environments
  • Success rates of different colony locations
  • Impact of urban development on bee populations
  • Effectiveness of conservation efforts

üå± How to Use NYC Feral Bee Survey

Adding a Marker

Click anywhere on the map to place a new marker. Choose the type (Hive, Swarm, Structure, or Tree), add notes, and optionally upload a photo.

Updating Status

Found that a swarm has moved on? Update the status to "Gone" to keep the data current and useful for the community.

Adding Comments

Share observations, updates, or additional information by commenting on existing markers.

Mobile Use

NYC Feral Bee Survey is optimized for mobile use. Perfect for field work - take photos and add markers while you're out and about!
