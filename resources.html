🚀 Quick Start Guide

New to NYC Feral Bee Survey? Get up and running in minutes with these essential steps:

🐝 Found a Swarm?

  1. Stay calm and keep a safe distance
  2. Take a photo if possible
  3. Click on our map to add the location
  4. Mark it as "Swarm" and add details
  5. Contact local beekeepers via New York Bee Club

📍 Adding Your First Marker

  1. Visit the NYC Feral Bee Survey Map
  2. Click anywhere on the map
  3. Choose marker type (Hive, Swarm, Structure, Tree)
  4. Add description and upload photo (optional)
  5. Submit to share with the community

📱 Mobile Tips

  • Enable location services for easier mapping
  • Take photos directly in the app
  • Update statuses on-the-go
  • Perfect for field work and hive inspections

🌱 Bee-Friendly Plants for NYC

Help support NYC's bee population by planting these bee-friendly species that thrive in urban environments:

Spring Bloomers

  • Crocus - Early nectar source
  • Pussy Willow - Pollen rich
  • Serviceberry - Native option
  • Redbud - Beautiful and functional
  • Tulip Trees - NYC favorite

Summer Favorites

  • Lavender - Drought tolerant
  • Sunflowers - High nectar/pollen
  • Bee Balm - Native wildflower
  • Echinacea - Long blooming
  • Black-eyed Susan - Urban hardy

Fall Support

  • Asters - Critical fall nectar
  • Goldenrod - Often blooms late
  • Sedum - Rooftop friendly
  • Joe Pye Weed - Native perennial
  • Japanese Knotweed - Controversial but valuable

💡 Urban Planting Tips

Focus on container gardening, rooftop spaces, and community gardens. Native plants are often more resilient in urban environments and provide better nutrition for local bee populations.
